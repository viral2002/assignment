heading tag

Heading tags in HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) are used to define headings and subheadings on a web page. They range from <h1> to <h6>, with <h1> being the most important and <h6> being the least important.

<h1> - Used for the main heading of the page, usually the largest and most important text on the page.</h1>
<h2> - Used for subheadings that are less important than the main heading.
<h3> - Used for sub-subheadings that are less important than <h2>.
<h4> - Used for sub-sub-subheadings that are less important than <h3>
<h5> - Used for sub-sub-sub-subheadings that are less important than <h4>.
<h6> - Used for the lowest level of subheadings, often used for minor sections or labels.

anchor tag

The anchor tag is one of the most important tags in HTML (Hypertext Markup Language) as it is used to create hyperlinks to other web pages, files, email addresses, and more. The anchor tag requires an href attribute, which specifies the destination of the link.

<a href="https://www.example.com">Visit Example </a>
